# Empirically Derived Coverage Thresholds

Computed via `pilot.py threshold` on `full_lhs.json` with `--exclude_models gemini-2.5-flash --n_permutations 10000`. The null distribution is generated by shuffling Democrat/Republican party labels within each specification (10,000 permutations) and recomputing the share of specifications producing a positive partisan gap.

## Headline thresholds (α = 0.05)

A reported finding's coverage must exceed the p95 of the null distribution to reject prompt-driven noise at α = 0.05.

| Item | Null p50 | **Null p95 (α = 0.05)** | Null p99 (α = 0.01) | Null max | Observed coverage | Margin over p95 |
|------|----------|-------------------------|----------------------|----------|--------------------|-----------------|
| Government spending | 0.480 | **0.516** | 0.530 | 0.564 | 0.998 | +48.2 pp |
| Immigration         | 0.477 | **0.513** | 0.529 | 0.563 | 0.998 | +48.5 pp |
| Gun control         | 0.476 | **0.510** | 0.526 | 0.553 | 0.868 | +35.8 pp |

## Interpretation

- All three observed coverages exceed even the α = 0.01 threshold (≤ 0.530 on every item). Directional consistency findings survive at the strictest conventional level.
- The α = 0.05 threshold averages **~0.513** across items — substantially below the 0.80 heuristic threshold proposed in the paper.
- Gun control sits 35.8 percentage points above its derived threshold; spending and immigration sit ~48 percentage points above theirs. This matches the paper's qualitative claim ("36 to 49 percentage points") with precise values.

## Suggested paper updates

The paper currently says (Section 1 / Section 4):

> "In our data that null sits near 51% on every item and observed coverage exceeds it by 36 to 49 percentage points."

Tighten to:

> "Permutation-derived α = 0.05 coverage thresholds are 0.516 (government spending), 0.513 (immigration), and 0.510 (gun control), each based on 10,000 within-specification party-label shuffles. Observed coverages (0.998, 0.998, 0.868) exceed the corresponding thresholds by 48.2, 48.5, and 35.8 percentage points respectively, and exceed the α = 0.01 thresholds (0.530, 0.529, 0.526) on every item."

For the reporting standard (Section 5+):

The 80% heuristic should be presented as a **practical robustness bar**, distinct from the α = 0.05 inferential threshold. A finding holding in ≥ 51% of specifications can be claimed as inferentially above chance; the 80% heuristic is a stronger, application-level bar for "robust." Reporting both is cleaner than reporting only one.

## Reproducibility

```bash
python3 pilot.py threshold \
  --output full_lhs.json \
  --exclude_models gemini-2.5-flash \
  --n_permutations 10000 \
  --seed 42
```

Wall-clock: ~3 minutes 37 seconds on M3 Pro.
